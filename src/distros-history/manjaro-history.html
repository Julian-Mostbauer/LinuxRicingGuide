Manjaro History

Manjaro is a user-friendly Linux distribution based on the Arch operating system. It provides all the benefits of the Arch operating system combined with a focus on user-friendliness and accessibility. Manjaro is suitable for both newcomers and experienced Linux users.

Year-by-Year Summary of Manjaro's History

  • 2011 – The Beginning

    Manjaro was founded by Roland Singer and Guillaume Benoit in 2011. The first version, Manjaro 0.1, was released in July 2011.

  • 2013 – Manjaro 0.8.0

    Manjaro 0.8.0 was released in August 2013, featuring a new graphical installer and improved hardware detection.

  • 2015 – Manjaro 15.09

    Manjaro 15.09, codenamed "Bellatrix", was released in September 2015, introducing the new Calamares installer and various performance improvements.

  • 2017 – Manjaro 17.0

    Manjaro 17.0, codenamed "Gellivara", was released in March 2017, featuring the latest Xfce and KDE Plasma desktop environments.

  • 2019 – Manjaro 18.0

    Manjaro 18.0, codenamed "Illyria", was released in October 2018, bringing updated software, new features, and improved hardware support.

  • 2021 – Manjaro 21.0

    Manjaro 21.0, codenamed "Ornara", was released in March 2021, featuring the latest desktop environments and various performance enhancements.

  • 2023 – Manjaro 23.0

    Manjaro 23.0 continued the tradition of providing a user-friendly and accessible Linux distribution with the latest software and features.
