Slackware History

Slackware is a Linux distribution created by Patrick Volkerding in 1993. Originally based on Softlanding Linux System, Slackware has been the basis for many other Linux distributions, most notably the first versions of SUSE Linux distributions, and is the oldest distribution that is still maintained.

Year-by-Year Summary of Slackware's History

  • 1993 – The Beginning

    Slackware was first released in July 1993 by Patrick Volkerding. It was based on the Softlanding Linux System (SLS) and aimed to provide a stable and easy-to-use Linux distribution.

  • 1995 – Slackware 3.0

    Slackware 3.0 was released in 1995, featuring the ELF binary format and support for the Linux 1.2.13 kernel.

  • 2000 – Slackware 7.1

    Slackware 7.1 was released in June 2000, featuring the Linux 2.2.16 kernel and various improvements to the system's performance and usability.

  • 2005 – Slackware 10.2

    Slackware 10.2 was released in September 2005, featuring the Linux 2.4.31 kernel and various updates to the system's software packages.

  • 2012 – Slackware 14.0

    Slackware 14.0 was released in September 2012, featuring the Linux 3.2.29 kernel and various improvements to the system's performance and stability.

  • 2016 – Slackware 14.2

    Slackware 14.2 was released in June 2016, featuring the Linux 4.4.14 kernel and various updates to the system's software packages.

  • 2021 – Slackware 15.0

    Slackware 15.0 was released in February 2021, featuring the Linux 5.10.16 kernel and various improvements to the system's performance, stability, and security.
