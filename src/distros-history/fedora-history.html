Fedora History

Fedora is a Linux distribution developed by the community-supported Fedora Project which is sponsored primarily by Red Hat, a subsidiary of IBM, with additional support from other companies. Fedora contains software distributed under various free and open-source licenses and aims to be on the leading edge of free technologies.

Year-by-Year Summary of Fedora's History

  • 2003 – The Beginning

    Fedora was first released in November 2003. It was created as a community-supported distribution sponsored by Red Hat.

  • 2005 – Fedora Core 4

    Fedora Core 4 was released in June 2005, featuring the GNOME 2.10 and KDE 3.4 desktop environments, and the inclusion of the Xen virtualization software.

  • 2007 – Fedora 7

    Fedora 7 was released in May 2007, merging the Fedora Core and Fedora Extras repositories, and introducing a new build system.

  • 2011 – Fedora 15

    Fedora 15 was released in May 2011, introducing the GNOME 3 desktop environment and the systemd init system.

  • 2014 – Fedora 21

    Fedora 21 was released in December 2014, introducing the Fedora.next initiative, which split Fedora into three distinct editions: Workstation, Server, and Cloud.

  • 2017 – Fedora 26

    Fedora 26 was released in July 2017, featuring the GNOME 3.24 desktop environment and various improvements to the system's performance and usability.

  • 2020 – Fedora 32

    Fedora 32 was released in April 2020, introducing the GNOME 3.36 desktop environment and various performance enhancements.

  • 2023 – Fedora 38

    Fedora 38 was released in April 2023, featuring the GNOME 44 desktop environment and various improvements to the system's performance, stability, and security.
