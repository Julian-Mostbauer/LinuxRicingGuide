openSUSE History

openSUSE is a Linux distribution sponsored by SUSE Software Solutions Germany GmbH and other companies. It is widely used throughout the world. The focus of its development is creating usable open-source tools for software developers and system administrators, while providing a user-friendly desktop and feature-rich server environment.

Year-by-Year Summary of openSUSE's History

  • 1994 – The Beginning

    openSUSE was first released in 1994 as S.u.S.E. Linux. It was created as a German translation of Slackware Linux.

  • 2005 – openSUSE Project

    In 2005, Novell announced the openSUSE Project, opening the development of SUSE Linux to the community.

  • 2010 – openSUSE 11.3

    openSUSE 11.3 was released in July 2010, featuring the KDE 4.4 and GNOME 2.30 desktop environments, and various improvements to the system's performance and usability.

  • 2015 – openSUSE Leap

    In 2015, openSUSE introduced Leap, a new version of openSUSE that shares a common code base with SUSE Linux Enterprise.

  • 2017 – openSUSE Leap 42.3

    openSUSE Leap 42.3 was released in July 2017, featuring the KDE Plasma 5.8 and GNOME 3.20 desktop environments, and various improvements to the system's performance and stability.

  • 2019 – openSUSE Leap 15.1

    openSUSE Leap 15.1 was released in May 2019, featuring the KDE Plasma 5.12 and GNOME 3.26 desktop environments, and various improvements to the system's performance and security.

  • 2021 – openSUSE Leap 15.3

    openSUSE Leap 15.3 was released in June 2021, featuring the KDE Plasma 5.18 and GNOME 3.34 desktop environments, and various improvements to the system's performance and usability.

  • 2023 – openSUSE Leap 15.5

    openSUSE Leap 15.5 was released in June 2023, featuring the KDE Plasma 5.24 and GNOME 3.38 desktop environments, and various improvements to the system's performance, stability, and security.
