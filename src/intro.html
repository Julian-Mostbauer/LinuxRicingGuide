Linux Ricing Guide - Introduction

What is Linux Ricing

Ricingis very similar to the modding or tuning community, the only difference is that Ricing is for your computer.

It is the process of customizing your desktop to make it look more visually appealing. This can be done by changing the theme, icons, fonts, and other visual elements of your desktop environment.

General overview:

Window-Manager/Desktop-Environment:

  • Users may want a lighter model like bspw. i3, bspwm, Openbox or more complex ones like GNOME or KDE Plasma.

Wallpaper:

  • Users can choose from a variety of wallpapers to customize their desktop.

Icons:

  • Users can change the icons on their desktop to make it look more visually appealing.

Fonts:

  • Users often choose specific Fonts, to underline the aesthetic of their system (for example Nerd Fonts or Powerline).

Themes:

  • GTK- or QT-Themes: Colors and Widgets
  • Icon-Packs: System-Icons.
  • Terminal-Themes: Colors and Fonts.
