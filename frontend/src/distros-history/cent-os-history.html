CentOS History

CentOS is a Linux distribution that provides a free, community-supported computing platform functionally compatible with its upstream source, Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL). In January 2014, CentOS announced the official joining with Red Hat while staying independent from RHEL, under a new CentOS governing board.

  • 2004 – The Beginning

    CentOS was first released in May 2004. It was created as a free alternative to Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL), providing the same functionality and stability.

  • 2006 – CentOS 4

    CentOS 4 was released in March 2006, based on RHEL 4. This release included various improvements and updates to the system's stability and performance.

  • 2011 – CentOS 6

    CentOS 6 was released in July 2011, based on RHEL 6. This release brought new features, improved performance, and enhanced security.

  • 2014 – CentOS joins Red Hat

    In January 2014, CentOS officially joined Red Hat while staying independent from RHEL. This move aimed to provide better support and resources for the CentOS community.

  • 2017 – CentOS 7

    CentOS 7 was released in July 2014, based on RHEL 7. This release included significant improvements to the system's performance, stability, and security.

  • 2020 – CentOS Stream

    In December 2020, CentOS announced the shift to CentOS Stream, a rolling-release distribution that sits between Fedora and RHEL, providing a preview of the next RHEL version.

  • 2021 – CentOS 8 End of Life

    In December 2021, CentOS 8 reached its end of life, marking the end of traditional CentOS releases and the full transition to CentOS Stream.
